About Me

My first ventures into the world of website creation were in 2006 when I taught myself HTML and CSS in order to create a website for my youth group. Even though the website was never published, I was instantly fascinated with the possibilities this technology offers. However it was not until my final years in university studying philosophy that I realized that I want to work in the field of web design and web development.

In the last years of my BA program I started dedicating more and more time towards learning web technologies and design principles. The year I graduated, I started freelancing and building websites for clients. Seeing this as the best decision of my career I have had a successful freelancing career since. Even though it has been a lot of hard work, overcoming many challenges and making and learning from many mistakes. I am in love with improving websites, user experiences and learning and applying new technologies.

I regard my unusual career path as an advantage. Studying Philosophy has had a huge impact in many areas of my daily work. Logic and analytical thinking helps me in programming as well as in designing front ends, Ethics defines the way I work as well as the way I communicate with and serve my clients and Epistemology is a huge asset when it comes to analyzing and optimizing user experience.

In the years to come I want to constantly keep learning, keep creating websites and share my enthusiasm with those who walk a similar path as I am.
